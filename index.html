Floral Harmony: The Jingdezhen Dish

Background

This delicate dish, one of a matched pair, offers a glimpse into the precision and beauty of Qing dynasty porcelain. The stylized floral motifs, rendered in dark cobalt glaze, dance across the shallow center and thick rim, exemplifying the celebrated craftsmanship of Jingdezhen, the porcelain capital of China.

Historical Context

Made during the late 17th to early 18th century, this dish reflects the artistic experimentation and mastery that Jingdezhen artisans were renowned for. Its simplicity belies a profound understanding of form, proportion, and decorative elegance, making it a favored piece in both imperial and diplomatic settings.

Provenance

Once part of Ambassador Jack Wilson Lydman's collection, this dish holds not only artistic value but also a legacy tied to cross-cultural appreciation and historical exchange.

Grace in Motion: The Courtly Baluster Vase

Background

This baluster vase, part of a five-piece garniture set, epitomizes the elegance of blue-and-white porcelain artistry from the Qing dynasty. Adorned with scenes of courtly entertainment, the vase features a noble lady surrounded by dancers and musicians, evoking the cultural sophistication of imperial China.

Historical Context

Crafted using underglaze cobalt blue on white porcelain, this vase reflects the technical mastery and aesthetic sensibilities of its time. Each brushstroke of the cobalt design speaks of refinement and storytelling, capturing moments of leisure and artistic tradition within the court.

Provenance

With a storied history of ownership, the vase has passed through esteemed collectors and galleries, including Duveen Brothers and Mrs. B.F. Jones, serving as a testament to its timeless appeal.

Garden Serenity: Beaker Vase from Qing Garniture

Background

This beaker-shaped vase, another gem from the five-piece garniture, presents a narrative of sophistication and artistic flair. Decorated with court scenes of refined elegance, the vase transitions from depictions of ladies in garden settings to herringbone borders.

Historical Context

Crafted with white porcelain and a clear feldspathic glaze, this vase represents the height of underglaze cobalt blue decoration. The artistic details capture not only the societal structures but also the technical ingenuity of the period.

Provenance

Like its counterpart, this vase has traveled through significant hands, including Duveen Brothers and Mrs. B.F. Jones, reflecting its enduring cultural and monetary value.
